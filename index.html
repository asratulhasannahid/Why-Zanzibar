The Case

Natural appeal reinforced by real fundamentals

Zanzibar's natural appeal is immediate — remarkable coastline, clear water, and a cultural identity that gives the island lasting global appeal. But for serious investors, beauty alone is never enough. What makes Zanzibar compelling is that its lifestyle value is now being reinforced by the factors that make a market feel safer to enter.

"This is not simply a beautiful island. It is a market where the fundamentals increasingly support the safety of the investment case as well as the upside."

Infrastructure

Built to receive the world

Stronger infrastructure reduces friction, improves accessibility, and supports long-term asset value. Zanzibar's gateway is already upgraded — and more is underway.

Air Access
Terminal 3 — AAKIA Airport

A new 21,153 m² international terminal opened in 2020, with further airport improvements underway to handle growing international passenger volumes.

Urban Investment
World Bank–Backed Development

Major investment supporting roads, drainage, public space, mobility, planning systems, and service delivery across the islands.

Legal Protections

Frameworks built for capital confidence

Recent reforms under the Tanzania Investment Act 2022 and the Zanzibar Investment Act 2023 were designed to make the environment more investment-friendly — with stronger institutions, appeals mechanisms, digitized processes, and one-stop facilitation.

Guarantees against nationalization and expropriation of investor assets
Free transfer of capital, profits, and dividends in convertible currency
Access to international arbitration including ICSID-based procedures
Granted benefits preserved for a defined period — not amended to investor's detriment
